WWE (World Wrestling Entertainment) is a global entertainment company that primarily focuses on professional wrestling. It was founded in 1952 by Jess McMahon and Toots Mondt. 
The company is currently owned and operated by Vince McMahon, who transformed it from a regional promotion to a worldwide phenomenon.
WWE produces various forms of entertainment content, including live events, television shows, films, merchandise, and digital media. Its flagship television programs are Raw and SmackDown, which feature professional wrestlers performing scripted matches and storylines. WWE also organizes pay-per-view events throughout the year, such as WrestleMania, Royal Rumble, and SummerSlam, which attract millions of viewers worldwide.
The WWE roster consists of both male and female wrestlers, known as Superstars, who compete in various divisions and championships. Some of the most notable WWE Superstars include John Cena, Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson, Hulk Hogan, Stone Cold Steve Austin, The Undertaker, and Becky Lynch.
In addition to wrestling, WWE has expanded into other forms of entertainment. It has its own film studio, WWE Studios, which produces and distributes movies featuring WWE Superstars. WWE has also ventured into reality television with shows like Total Divas and Miz & Mrs.
WWE has a rich history and has played a significant role in popularizing professional wrestling as a form of entertainment. It has a large and passionate fanbase known as the WWE Universe, who actively engage with the product through live events, social media, and merchandise sales.
